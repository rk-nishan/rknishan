About Me

Microbiologist & Computational Biology Researcher exploring disease mechanisms, therapeutic targets, and pathogen identification.

I am an MPhil researcher in Microbiology at Jagannath University, Dhaka. My research lies at the intersection of molecular diagnostics, vaccine design, and computational biology. Currently, my thesis project focuses on the development of a strip-based DNA hybridization assay for the rapid, cost-effective detection of selected Gram-positive nosocomial pathogens and their virulence/resistance markers.

Over the past few years, I have actively expanded my research to incorporate computational biology workflows. I specialize in applying statistical programming (R and Python) to analyze multi-omics data, construct ceRNA interaction networks, run survival modeling, perform molecular docking simulations, and design broad-spectrum multi-epitope vaccines against major clinical pathogens such as Streptococcus pneumoniae and the Nipah virus.

I have served as a Visiting Graduate Researcher at the Bangladesh Council of Scientific and Industrial Research (BCSIR) and as a Research Assistant in the Department of Microbiology at Jagannath University. My goal is to bridge the gap between traditional wet-lab assays and modern bioinformatics pipelines to discover actionable clinical solutions.
